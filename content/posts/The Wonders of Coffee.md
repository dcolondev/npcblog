## History of Coffee

Coffee originated in Ethiopia, and the legend has it that a goat herder named Kaldi discovered it. He noticed that his goats became very energetic after eating the berries of a particular plant. ## Types of Coffee Beans

There are two main types of coffee beans: Arabica and Robusta. Arabica beans have a mild, sweeter taste and a higher acidity level. They are usually more expensive than Robusta beans, which have a stronger, more bitter taste and a higher caffeine content.

## Brewing Methods

There are numerous ways to brew coffee, including drip, French press, espresso, and pour-over. Each method has its own unique taste and aroma.

### Drip

Drip coffee is common in households and offices. It involves pouring hot water over ground coffee beans using a drip coffee machine.

### French Press

French press coffee involves steeping coarse coffee grounds in hot water, then using a plunger to filter the coffee.

### Espresso

Espresso is a highly concentrated and flavorful coffee that is brewed by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee beans under high pressure.

### Pour-Over

Pour-over is becoming a popular brewing method in recent years, which involves slowly pouring hot water over coffee grounds. It is known for its simplicity and purity.
